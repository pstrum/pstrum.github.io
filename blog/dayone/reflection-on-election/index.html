Realization

Today I woke up in a country where a racist, bigot, xenophobe, misogynist, sexist, narcissistic asshole was elected to be the president. More than half the country chose a person with those values to preside over us.

A person (thing) with those values has no authority over me.

I broke down when the weight of knowing that more than half the people in my country are racists. Why are they so fearful? How can someone see people that are different from them and feel hatred? Not only hatred, but the drive to hurt them?

More than half of my country is racist white people.

What is truly frightening is that now they have a leader. He makes me sick, and thinking of those that voted for him makes my stomach hurt.

If you voted for a man who is blatantly all those things, then you yourself are just like him: a racist, bigot, xenophobe, misogynist.

You are a racist if you believe you are superior to other races.
You are a misogynist if you show prejudice against women.
You are a xenophobe if you fear people from other countries.
You are a bigot if you are intolerant of those with different opinions.
You are sexist if discriminate other genders.

When I read those descriptions, I think to myself how they all shout FEAR! These people are afraid of what is different, and want to show power over what they fear.

Not me. That is the opposite of who I am.

Donald Trump, our President, is all of those things. His supporters celebrate those qualities. They are the majority in my country.

Now it is up to us, the people who look at what Donald Trump is and want to puke (hardcore), to help the majority understand their fears of what is different are because they are uneducated. They fear what they don’t know and understand. And if it is too late for them, we must know that it isn’t too late for their children.

Educate, educate, educate.

Ask yourself today, how are you going to help? How are you going to help eliminate hatred in our world?
