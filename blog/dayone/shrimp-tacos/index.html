Shrimp Tostadas with Mango Salsa

I made this recipe tonight pretty quick, maybe 45 minutes total. It basically combines our favorite chili (paprika) lime shrimp on top of a mango salsa (from our fish tacos) and a crispy corn tortilla (from our chicken tostadas). The combination is really quite awesome, though a bit crumbly and messy! A couple important notes are to use plenty of veggie oil, heat the oil pretty good so the tortillas cook quick, buy deveined shrimp if possible, and chop the salsa veggies nice and small.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 8 corn tortillas
  • 1 mango
  • 1 avocado
  • 1/2 cup red onion
  • 2 vine tomatoes
  • 1 jalapeño
  • 2 tablespoons cilantro
  • 1.5 limes juiced
  • 1 teaspoon champagne vinegar
  • 1 lb shrimp
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3/4 teaspoon paprika
  • salt
  • pepper

Directions

Heat a small skillet and add 1/4 of vegetable oil, allow oil to get hot.

Cook corn tortillas in hot oil, about 3 minutes per side, until nice and crispy. Remove each tortilla and place onto a paper towel lined plate.
Meanwhile, peel and finely chop the mango, red onion, and avocado. Add them to a medium mixing bowl.

Rinse and finely chop the tomatoes, jalapeño, and cilantro. Mix with mango, red onion, and avocado.

Squeeze the juice of one lime into the salsa mixture and pour in a small amount of champagne vinegar.

Add salt and pepper to taste and mix the salsa together.

Peel, devein, and rinse shrimp. Sprinkle with salt and pepper.

Head olive oil in a deep skillet, add garlic and sauté about 1 minute.

Add shrimp to pan and cool about 4 minutes per side until white through the entire body.

Add paprika and the juice of 1/2 a lime to the shrimp and stir to combine.

Plate by topping crispy tostada with salsa and then 3 shrimp.
